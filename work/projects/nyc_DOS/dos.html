Project History
New York City's Department of Sanitation is one of the world's largest, collecting over 12,000 tons of residential and institutional refuse and recyclables each day. As such, the agency's pursuit of a high-profile site in downtown Manhattan for its new facility requested an innovative expression and a forward-thinking design sympathetic to its context.
The resultant design for the 100,000-square-foot building is a composition of forms, taking cues from the various program functions and the local built environment.
A naturally lit atrium, rooftop terrace, and maximized glazed walls capitalize on the river view.
Solar control devices and systems achieve desired energy and support design efforts to provide a safe and positive work environment

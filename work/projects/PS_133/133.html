Project History
To serve the expanding student population in the surrounding districts, SBLM was commisioned to design a new school facility to house two district primary schools within one footprint accomodating 950 students. The new 116,000-square-foot building is located on the perimeter of the existing parcel of land, originally allocated for a playground and community garden. Additionally the design for the new PS 133K pays homage to the original structure by incorporating exterior elements that recall the Flemish Renaissance style, including steeply gabled parapet roos and ornamental brickwork which were used as inspiration for the new school.
An architectural icon in the surrounding community, the original school was designed in the early 1900's in the Flemish Renaissance style. Working closely with the NYCSCA and the State Historic Preservation Office, SBLM identified building elements to be salvaged and integrated into the design of the new facility.
The new facility responds to the surrounding low-rise residential neighborhood by locating the greatest density of development along the site's commercial street, and relocating the community garden as buffer between the residential neighbors and the school's main play yard.
The building features a full size gymnasium, auditorium, cafeteria along with art, music, and science rooms and meets the NYCSCA's Green School Guidelines.

Project History
In order to maximize the three stories and 75,000 square-feet irregulary shaped site, SBLM used a linear building to play a significant role in the urban context. This urban association is at the heart of the client's brief, and keeps within the sustainable initiatives and goals outlined for the project as it creates a pedestrian-friendly foyer and eliminates the need for a supplementary security fence.
The site stands on a 300-acre parcel of land on the scenic Brooklyn waterfront. Designed to achieve LEED-Silver at minimum, the design proposes several Green features that speak to the client's desire for a forward thining design on a minimal budget.
The composition features white-roof technolo sgy, which supplies similar benefits of a green roof without the associates costs required to structurally support it.
